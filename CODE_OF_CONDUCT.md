# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in Rosetta Bridge a harassment-free experience for everyone, including SDK users, adapter authors, maintainers, integrators, and security researchers, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Scope

This code applies to Rosetta Bridge issues, pull requests, discussions, security reports, maintainer communication, and shared support spaces. It also applies when someone represents the project in public community settings.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints, workflows, and production contexts
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the primitive's users
- Respecting private provider settings, customer prompts, signed asset URLs, provider payloads, internal provider IDs, route handlers, and deployment details

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery in community spaces
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, provider credentials, customer prompts, signed asset URLs, provider payloads, internal provider IDs, route handlers, or vulnerability details without explicit permission
- Pressuring maintainers or contributors to disclose secrets, private customer data, private generated media, or provider details

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the maintainers at `dev@babysea.ai`. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Do not post private provider credentials, customer prompts, signed asset URLs, production provider payloads, internal provider IDs, route handlers, or unreleased vulnerability details in public issues or discussions. Use the private vulnerability reporting flow described in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) for sensitive security or schema-boundary concerns.

Maintainers may remove comments, close discussions, block accounts, or take other reasonable action when behavior does not meet this code of conduct.
